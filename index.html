I am a Ph.D. student at OSU SunLab advised by Prof. Huan Sun. My research focuses on Natural Language Processing, Conversational AI, and Human-Computer Interaction. I'm currently interested in:
  • Interactive Language Agents: Large langauge models (LLMs) have achieved astonishing results in various tasks. I'm particularly interested in buiding language agents with LLMs that can autonomously plan for their tasks, refine their answers, and memorize task-specific knowledge. Moreover, I value the human-centered nature of language agents: although they excel at instruction following, how can we enable them to proactively collaborate with human users to make decisions? When would users prefer such agents over other mature (e.g. GUI-based) tools for their tasks, such as database browsing and web navigation?
  • Continual Learning: The development of language agents is never a one-time effort. After the initial depolyment, how can we teach models to solve new problems via trial-and-error and learn from their mistakes like humans? How to adapt models to novel language uses, especially those not covered in the training corpus? How to turn general agents into more personalized assistants according to the users' preference?
  • Reliability and Explainability: While LLMs are powerful, they also make silly mistakes here and there. How can we enable models to better calibrate their predictions and explain them in natural language? How to prevent LLMs from giving undesired responses (e.g. misinformation, hallucination, social bias)?
I am also interested in human languages themselves and philosophy. So far, I can speak Chinese, English, Japanese (JLPT N2), and some elementary Korean. My favorite quote is “Cogito, ergo sum.” (or in English, “I think. Therefore, I am.”) by René Descartes.
