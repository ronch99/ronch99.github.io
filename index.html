I am a Ph.D. student at OSU SunLab advised by Prof. Huan Sun. My research focuses on Natural Language Processing, Conversational AI, and Machine Learning. I'm particularly interested in developing practical, reliable, and generalizable langauge agents that can accomplish real-world tasks by processing heterogeneous data, planning and reasoning in programs, and collaborating with users:
  • My approach to LLM research is to treat the models as black-boxs and study their behaviors by manimulating data, training algorithms, and inference methods. Recently, I am actively thinking about improving LLMs' inference-time computation, such as iterative refinement, tree search, and hierarchical planning.
  • I prefer principled agent designs that are simple, effective, and cost-efficient over complex frameworks with many modules and pipelines. I believe we should grasp a decent understanding of each component and method, e.g. discriminator, grounding unit, and planning method, before incorporating them into an agent.
  • The development of language agents is never a one-time effort. I'm interested in developing new algorithms that enable agents to continually learn from human, AI, and environment feedback, generalize beyond their initial training corpus, and provide personalized experiences for their users.
I am also interested in human languages themselves. So far, I can speak Chinese, English, Japanese (JLPT N2), and some elementary Korean.
